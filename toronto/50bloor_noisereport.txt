EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report has been prepared as part of the approvals subm ission for the proposed m ixed-use developm ent
located at 50 Bloor Street W est in the City of Toronto. The project consists of a 75-storey tower rising from
an 8-storey podium , fronting onto Bloor Street W est, for a total of 83-storeys. The podium building will contain
com m ercial and office uses. Parts of the podium building rooftop will be available for am enity space for the
residential use.

The transportation noise sources with potential for adverse noise im pact on the proposed developm ent are
the road traffic on Bloor Street W est, Bay Street and Yonge Street.

The sound exposures on site have been determ ined and com pared with the applicable Ministry of the
Environm ent (MOE) and City of Toronto noise guidelines to determ ine the need for noise m itigation. To m eet
the applicable transportation noise source guideline lim its:

·        All residential dwelling units require m andatory air conditioning.

·        The applicable indoor noise guidelines at all buildings are predicted to be m et without any special
         window upgrades beyond the m inim um non-acoustical requirem ents of the Ontario Building Code
         (OBC) based on the assum ption that STC 54 exterior wall construction will be provided. Final
         requirem ents should be checked when detailed building plans are available.

1.0      INTRODUCTION

Valcoustics Canada Ltd. was retained to prepare an environm ental noise feasibility study for the proposed
m ixed-use developm ent at 50 Bloor Street W est, in support of an application for zoning by-law am endm ent
to the City of Toronto. The proposed developm ent will consist of a high-rise residential tower, a podium
building with com m ercial uses and an underground parking garage.

The surrounding area consists m ainly of existing com m ercial (retail) and office uses, with som e existing and
anticipated future residential developm ents nearby. The site is bounded by:

·        Bloor Street W est with com m ercial developm ents to the south;
·        existing com m ercial high-rise with Yonge Street to the east;
·        existing com m ercial high-rise with Bay Street to the west; and
·        existing com m ercial developm ent with Mayfair Mews and Cum berland Street to the north.

3.0      ENVIRONMENTAL NOISE CRITERIA

3.1      M OE ­ ROAD TRAFFIC

The environm ental noise guidelines of the MOE outlined in LU-131, "Noise Assessment Criteria in Land Use
Planning", are applicable to this site and are sum m arized in Appendix B.

3.1.1    Indoors

In accordance with the MOE practice, if the daytim e sound energy exposure due to road traffic, L eq Day(1), at
the exterior face of living/dining room s is greater than 65 dBA, or if the nighttim e sound energy exposure (due
to road traffic), L eq Night(2), at the exterior face of bedroom s is greater than 60 dBA, m eans m ust be provided
so that windows can be kept closed for noise control purposes and central air conditioning is required. For
daytim e sound exposures between 56 and 65 dBA inclusive, or for nighttim e sound exposures between
51 and 60 dBA inclusive, there need only be the provision for adding air conditioning at a later date, at the
occupant's discretion. A warning clause advising the occupants of the potential interference with som e
activities is also required.

For indoor areas, the daytim e guidelines for living and dining room s is 45 dBA (L eq Day) for road traffic sound
sources. The nighttim e guideline for bedroom s is 40 dBA (L eq Night) for road traffic sound sources.

3.1.2    Outdoors

For outdoor am enity areas ("Outdoor Living Areas" ­ OLA), the guideline is 55 dBA L eq Day (0700 to
2300 hours), with an excess not exceeding 5 dBA considered acceptable. If it is technically not practicable
to achieve the 55 dBA objective, warning clauses are required to be registered on title. Note that for road

·        at least 4 m in depth;
·        outside the building facade; and
·        unenclosed.

4.0      NOISE IMPACT ASSESSMENT

Using the road traffic data in Table 1, the sound energy exposures, in term s of L eq Day and L eq Night, were
determ ined using STAMSON V5.04 ­ ORNAMENT, the com puterized road traffic noise prediction m odel of
the MOE.

Inherent screening of each building face due to its orientation to the noise source was taken into account.
To be conservative, screening by the existing intervening buildings was not accounted for.

For the daytim e and nighttim e sound exposures, the receptors were taken at top storey windows (worst case).
For all practical purposes, these sound levels are representative of traffic noise over the vertical extent of the
building facades.

Table 2 sum m arizes the predicted sound exposures at various locations as shown on Figure 2. A sam ple
sound exposure calculation is contained in Appendix C.

The highest sound exposures of 71 dBA daytim e and 65 dBA nighttim e due to road traffic are predicted to
occur at Locations G and H (south facade).

Truck activity along the northern lane was assessed on a worst case basis using survey data provided by Lea
Consulting Ltd., resulting in 60 dBA at the closest residential windows.

requirem ents. W all and window areas were assumed to be 80% and 30%, respectively, of their associated
floor area on the facades directly and partially exposed to the noise source(s). It is also assum ed that STC 54
exterior wall construction will be provided.

6.0      THE EFFECT OF THE PROJECT ON THE NEIGHBOURHOOD

The m ain source of noise associated with this developm ent, with the potential for adverse im pact on
surrounding buildings, is the m echanical equipm ent. These system s are not yet designed.

Based on prelim inary m echanical concepts prepared by The Mitchell Partnership Inc., received on
March 8, 2012, m echanical/electrical room s will be located at P6, Mezzanine Level, 8 th floor, 39 th floor and
Penthouse. Louvres for the generators, ventilation units and cooling towers will be located on the north side
at Mezzanine Level and 8 th floor.

Garage exhaust fans with openings at grade level are also a potential noise sources.

Mechanical equipm ent interfacing to the outdoors m ust com ply with Chapter 591 of the City of Toronto
Municipal Code. By proper engineering design, all requirem ents can be met and no adverse noise im pact
would be created for surrounding uses. Appropriate choice of location, equipm ent type, and noise control
features, such as silencers and sound barriers should be considered during detailed design.

For exam ple, for cooling towers and fluid coolers (8 th floor), a new generation of very quiet fans are available.
In addition, the use of energy efficient variable frequency drives is also very useful in m inim izing noise
generation. Air handling system s, at any level, including exhaust fans, that need to interface to the exterior
environm ent, can be fitted with silencers. The equipm ent room layouts need to anticipate the space
requirem ents to accom m odate these devices. Sim ilarly, em ergency power generators need significant
am ounts of cooling and com bustion air from the outside. Again, accom m odation of potentially large silencers
on air intakes and exhausts m ust be m ade in the detailed design. In the case of underground garage exhaust
fans, it m ay be necessary to use centrifugal fans as opposed to the m ore com m only used propeller fans, to
accom m odate silencers, if necessary. For em ergency power (diesel or natural gas) generators, in excess of
700 kW , an Environm ental Com pliance Approval is required from the MOE, under Part 9 of the Environm ental
Protection Act, addressing noise and air quality. For standby power generators of 700 kW or less, the
installation m ust be filed with the MOE Registry and m ust com ply with Ontario Regulation 245/11, prom ulgated
in June 2011. For generators within a building, am ongst other requirem ents O.Reg. 245/11 sets out m inim um
silencing requirem ents for cooling and exhaust air paths and engine exhaust m uffler system s. By these

7.0         CONCLUSIONS

The findings of this report indicate that the applicable MOE road traffic noise guidelines can be m et and a
suitable acoustical environm ent provided for the occupants. The road traffic noise exposures and attendant
noise m itigation requirem ents are typical of m any other m ixed use/residential developm ents adjacent to m ajor
arterial roadways in the GTA. W ith appropriate design of the developm ent, a suitable acoustical environm ent
can be provided for the occupants and the applicable MOE noise guideline requirem ents m et. Future
occupants will be m ade aware of the potential noise situation through warning clauses, as per MOE
guidelines.

All residential units will be outfitted with central air conditioning. W indow construction m eeting the m inim um
non-acoustical standards of the OBC will allow the indoor noise guidelines to be m et for all the residential units
based on assum ed window and wall areas as well as STC 54 exterior wall construction. These aspects
should be reviewed once the building plans are available. This review is usually done as part of the building
perm it application process.

The noise sources, such as the m echanical equipm ent and generator, associated with the proposed
developm ent m ust com ply with Chapter 591 of the City of Toronto Municipal Code. By proper engineering
design, all requirem ents can be m et and no adverse noise im pact would be created for the surrounding uses.

The approvals and adm inistrative procedures are available to ensure that any acoustical requirem ents are
im plem ented.
