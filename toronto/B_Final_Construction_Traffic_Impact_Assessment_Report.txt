1.        INTRODUCTION AND SCOPE OF WORK
The scope of this traffic assessment relates to planned construction activities during tunneling operation
and subway station construction. The tunneling is scheduled to begin with the Advanced Launch shaft
construction in 2010 with simultaneous construction of station box and the building structure. The
construction of the advanced launch shaft is to assist tunnel operation which is to begin in 2010 and last
through mid 2014 with simultaneous construction of the subway station. It is assumed that construction
activities will reach their maximum during this period with both tunneling operation and subway box
construction.

In our traffic analysis, we estimated the total trip generation for the proposed construction and
summarized the traffic impacts on the surrounding road network with specific focus on the following two
signalized intersections located at the east and west of the existing Canadian National (CN) tracks bridge.

      •   Sheppard Avenue and Chesswood Drive
      •   Sheppard Avenue and Tuscan Gate

Figure 1 shows these intersections and the general study area.



2.        STUDY APPROACH AND METHODOLOGY
2.1       Analysis Period
The analysis period was considered in conjunction with the AM and PM peak hours of the adjacent street
traffic thus representing a “Worst Case Scenario”. The AM and PM peak hours (noted below) for the
analyzed intersections were obtained from the turning movement counts provided by the City.

      •   AM peak hour: 7:45 – 8:45
      •   PM peak hour: 4:15 – 5:15

The working weekday AM and PM peak periods were used for the analysis.

2.2       Analysis Methodology
To quantify the traffic impacts of the construction activities on the operational performance of the nearby
intersections, the study area intersections mentioned above have been analyzed considering the three
scenarios listed below:

      •   Existing (2010) traffic conditions,
      •   Future background traffic conditions (without traffic contributions from the proposed construction);
          and
      •   Future total traffic conditions (with traffic contributions from the proposed construction work)

Assuming that the construction activities will peak sometime between the period of 2010 and 2014, 2014
was considered as the future analysis year to assess the impact of the construction activities on the
nearby intersections.

The operation of the study area network was analyzed using Synchro/SimTraffic v.7, which employs the
analysis procedures from the Highway Capacity Manual for signalized and unsignalized intersections.
The saturation flow rate of 1900 and a PHF of 0.92 were considered for the analysis. The measures such

as volume to capacity ratio (V/C ratio), control delay and critical movement Level of Service (LOS) were
used to quantify the impacts.

Level of Service (LOS) is a measure used to describe the operating characteristics of an intersection or a
section of roadway. There are six levels ranging from A (excellent – low delay) to E (capacity – very long
delays) and F (failure or forced flow). Typically, LOS C or D is considered an acceptable level of operation
for urban conditions and for the design of future road improvements. LOS definitions for signalized and
unsignalized intersections are attached as Appendix A.



3.        EXISTING CONDITION
3.1       Description of Study Area Intersections
Located east of the existing CN tracks, Sheppard Avenue and Chesswood Drive is a three-leg signalized
intersection. There are two through lanes for east and west approaches. The eastbound approach
includes a third exclusive left turn lane, while the westbound right turn is shared with one through lane.
On the north leg, there are two separate lanes for southbound left turn and southbound right turn.

Sheppard Avenue and Tuscan Gate is a signalized intersection located west of the CN tracks. Sheppard
Avenue is a regional arterial road with a four-lane cross section which is oriented east-west at this
intersection. Oriented north-south is Tuscan Gate which serves as a minor entrance road to Downsview
Park. The eastbound movement constitute an exclusive left turn and two through lane one with shared
right turn. The westbound movement has two through lanes and exclusive lanes for both left and right
turn traffic. For southbound traffic, a provision is made for a shared right, through and left turn lane while
the northbound movement constitutes an exclusive left turn lane and a through lane with a shared right
turn.

Existing lane configurations of the two intersections are shown in Figure 2.

3.2       Existing Traffic Volume
The study team was provided with the most recent (2005-2006) 8-hour turning movement counts along
with the AM and PM peak hour traffic for the study area intersections. These counts were projected to
2010 volume to represent the existing condition using a 0.5% compounded growth rate per year. Figure 3
shows the existing traffic volumes assumed in our analysis. All the turning movement counts provided by
the City are attached as Appendix B.

4.               FUTURE CONDITION (CONSTRUCTION PERIOD)
4.1              Future Background Traffic Volume
AS previously indicated, year 2014 was considered as the future analysis year in assessing the impacts
of proposed construction activities on future traffic conditions. Future background traffic forecasts have
been prepared to reflect the future year traffic. To estimate the future background traffic, which is defined
as traffic excluding those generated by the proposed construction work, a 0.5% growth rate per year
related to background population and employment growth was considered to project the existing volume
to reflect the traffic volume in 2014. This growth rate was compounded annually and applied to existing
2010 traffic volumes to reflect general increases in traffic in the year 2014. The resulting 2014
background traffic volumes are illustrated in Figure 4.

Drive will operate with an acceptable LOS C and D respectively during the AM and PM peak hours but

4.3              Proposed Development Details and Site Traffic
The proposed Sheppard West Station is located within Parc Downsview Park. Figure 1 shows the
location of the proposed subway station and the access points for the construction traffic. It is our
understanding that the tunneling schedule would begin with the Advanced Launch shaft construction in
2010 and will continue simultaneously with construction of station box and building structure, and is
expected to be completed in 2014. It is assumed that the construction activity will reach its maximum
during this period with both tunneling operation and subway box construction.

4.3.1            Trip Generation
Based on the memo provided by Hatch Mott MacDonald (Appendix C) for the tunneling operation, and
estimated operations regarding the construction of the subway box, the proposed operation will consist of
several elements which will contribute to construction traffic. Construction traffic related to spoil haulage
and construction consumables for the tunneling operation and the construction of the subway box make
up the majority of the traffic volumes.

The tunnel operation will generate a total of about 25 trucks per hour over an estimated 8 hour hauling
window. The anticipated number of trucks and cars generated from the proposed construction work is

4.3.2   Trip Distribution and Assignment
Three different scenarios were considered to distribute the site-generated traffic to the adjacent road
network and to examine the impact of site-generated traffic to the study intersections.

Scenario 1

    •   50% of both car and truck trips will be coming in from the east of the intersection of Sheppard and
        Chesswood and 50% from the south of the intersection of Sheppard and Tuscan Gate. The same
        distribution is assumed for trips leaving the sites.

Scenario 2

    •   100% of both car and truck trips will be coming from east of the intersection of Sheppard and
        Chesswood during the AM and PM peak periods
    •   50% will leave through the intersection of Sheppard and Chesswood while 50% through the
        intersection Sheppard and Tuscan during both AM and PM peak periods.

Scenario 3

    •   100% of both cars and truck trips will be coming from south of the intersection (Sheppard and
        Tuscan) during AM and PM periods
    •   50% will leave through the intersection of Sheppard and Chesswood and 50% through Sheppard
        and Tuscan during AM and PM peak periods.

The overall trip distribution for the site-generated traffic is illustrated in Table 6.

5.                   Study Findings and Recommendations

This study assessed the traffic impacts of expected construction activities related to the proposed subway
station within the vicinity of Parc Downsview Park with particular emphasis on the two nearby
intersections including Shepherd Avenue at Chesswood Drive and Tuscan Gate. The findings of this
study are summarized below:

5.1                  Study Findings
5.1.1                Existing Traffic Condition
Under the existing traffic conditions, the overall LOS for the intersection of Sheppard Avenue and
Chesswood Drive are assessed at LOS C and LOS D for AM and PM peak periods. As depicted in
Table 1, all the movements are operating at a reasonable LOS E or better, except the following:

      •              During AM peak hour, eastbound left turn movement at the intersection of Sheppard Avenue and
                     Chesswood Drive show a V/C ratio of 1.13, with a failing LOS F;

                                                                                                                                   Page 13
                                                         Construction Traffic Impact Assessment Report
                                                               Toronto York Spadina Subway Extension
                                                                                 Sheppard West Station

    •   During PM peak hour, southbound right turn movements at the intersection of Sheppard Avenue
        and Chesswood Drive show a V/C ratio of 1.10, with a failing LOS F;

The Intersection of Sheppard Avenue and Tuscan Gate will operate at acceptable LOS C and D during
peak hours with the following exception:

    •   During the PM peak hour, the shared southbound left/through/right movement at the intersection
        of Sheppard Avenue and Tuscan Gate will operate at LOS F with a V/C ratio of 1.29.
 

5.1.2   Future Background Traffic Condition
Under the future 2014 background traffic condition, the intersection of Sheppard Avenue and Chesswood
Drive will fail (LOS F) during the PM peak hour but will operate with good LOS C during the AM peak
hour. Southbound right turn movement of this intersection will fail during the PM peak hour traffic
condition.

The Intersection of Sheppard Avenue and Tuscan Gate will operate at good LOS C and D during the AM
and PM peak periods with the exception of the southbound movement which is expected to operate at
LOS F during the PM peak period.

5.1.3   Future Traffic Condition
Under future total traffic condition considering Scenario 1 site traffic distribution, the intersection of
Sheppard Avenue and Chesswood Drive will fail (LOS F) during the PM peak hour but will operate with a
LOS D during the AM peak hour. Also, eastbound left movements during both AM and PM peak hour,
westbound left/through/right movement during the PM peak hour at this intersection will fail (LOS F). The
intersection of Sheppard Avenue and Tuscan Gate will operate at good LOS C and D during both AM and
PM peak periods. However, the southbound shared left/through/right movement of this intersection will
fail during the PM peak period. This movement is already failing under the future background traffic
condition indicating that the construction site traffic would have minimal impacts on this intersection.

Under future total traffic conditions with Scenario 2 site traffic distribution, the intersection of Sheppard
Avenue and Chesswood Drive will fail (LOS F) during the PM peak hour but it will operate at good LOS D
during the AM peak hour. Also, at this intersection, the westbound left/through/right movement during the
PM peak hour and eastbound left movement during both AM and PM peak hour will fail (LOS F). With
some few exceptions the Intersection of Sheppard Avenue and Tuscan Gate will operate at good LOS
(LOS C and D) during both the AM and PM peak period. But at this intersection the shared southbound
left/through/right movement will fail during the PM peak hour period. This movement is already packed
with the future background traffic. Thus, traffic generated from construction site will have minimal impacts
on this intersection.

Under the future total traffic condition with Scenario 3 site traffic distribution, the intersection of Sheppard
Avenue and Chesswood Drive will fail (LOS F) during the PM peak hour but it will operate with a good
LOS C during the AM peak hour. Also, westbound left/through/right movement during PM, eastbound left
movement during both AM and the PM peak hour for this intersection will fail (LOS F). The Intersection of
Sheppard Avenue and Tuscan Gate will operate at LOS C and D during the AM and PM peak periods
respectively. But at this intersection the shared southbound left/through/right turn movement will fail
during the PM peak period. This movement is already packed with the future background traffic indicating
that traffic generated from the construction site will have minimal impacts on this intersection

5.2       Recommendations
The results of our traffic assessment indicate that the overall traffic impacts of the planned construction
activities on the nearby intersections analyzed may be minimal. Some of the movements which are found
unacceptable or failing under the LOS criteria for the future (2014) traffic conditions (including the
construction traffic) are also failing under the future background traffic condition (excluding the
construction traffic). The following are some recommendations to accommodate the future background
traffic as well as the site generated traffic from the proposed construction work. The future lane
configurations are shown in Figure 14.

Intersection of Sheppard Avenue and Chesswood Drive

      •   Increase signal timing to 120 sec for PM peak period.

Intersection of Sheppard Avenue and Tuscan Gate

      •   Add exclusive southbound left turn lane

The proposed exclusive southbound left turn lane requirement at the intersection of Sheppard Avenue
and Tuscan Gate is not related to the construction traffic. This left turn lane has been recommended to
improve the existing and future background traffic operations, since the shared (left/through/right)
movement is operating at LOS F in the existing traffic condition and is expected to operate at LOS F in
the future background traffic condition as well.

It is worth noting that increase in signal timing as proposed for the intersection of Sheppard Avenue and
Chesswood Drive may cause some delays to the other intersection within the proximity of this
intersection. In that case, the City may wish to proceed with this recommendation following a thorough
investigation of the operational impacts.

3.       Segment transport
Segments are delivered to the tunnelling sites to keep pace with the tunnelling operation. On
average, this will be 10 rings per day. We believe that the segment manufacturer will be
transporting two complete rings in each load. The anticipated truck numbers are thus as
follows:

      Planned advance rate, m/d               15
      PCTL Ring Width, m                   1.500
      Average rings per day per drive       10.0
      Average rings per truck                   2
      Number of trucks per day per drive        5
      Average trucks per hour per drive       0.4 hauling 12h/day

Where, the contractor is driving two TBMs at a time, the number of segment trucks would
average 10 per day over an assumed 12 hour hauling window.


4.       Other tunnelling consumables
Regular delivery of the following consumables is typically required for EPB TBM tunnelling
operations:

     •   Vent line – will be extended by cartridge on average once per week,
     •   Mine track – approximately 12 – 6 m lengths required per day for twin drives,
     •   Water and air pipe,
     •   Electrical equipment,
     •   Pumps,
     •   Grease and oil,
     •   Spoil additives.
Delivery of the consumables above can be stocked on-site, and would be covered by 1 to 2
trucks per week.

4.1      Bulk Materials
It is assumed that a grout batch plant will be located on-site at the launch locations for annulus
grouting of the segmental lining. Prepared grout will be conveyed to the TBM. Sand and cement
for the grout mix will be delivered to the batch plant. It is anticipated that the average daily
consumption of sand and cement for twin tunnel drives is approximately 20 m3 and 2.5 m3
respectively. It is therefore reasonable to assume 2 trucks per day for sand and 1 truck per
week for cement.

5.         Irregular construction vehicles
Irregular vehicle traffic at the sites are typically comprised of (but not limited to):

     •     Road washing/sweeping – spoil haulage operations will necessitate some form of road
           washing/sweeping to varying degrees depending on site location,
     •     Miscellaneous materials,
     •     Vacuum trucks for maintenance of environmental controls.
These activities could comprise up to 2 trucks/vehicles per day for twin tunnel drives.


6.         TBM set-up
TBMs are shipped to site in large components. These typically arrive on special trailers and are
offloaded directly into the shaft where the TBM is assembled. Contractors usually hire a high
capacity crane for this operation, typically in the 450 to 500 t size range. This offloading
operation can take up to two weeks. TBM commissioning takes longer: usually six to eight
weeks.


7.         Cars
Traffic movements associated with commuting of the labour force are grouped around shift
change times. There are planned to be two 10-hour tunnelling shifts and a single maintenance
shift, five days per week. In addition, there are staff supporting the construction on the
